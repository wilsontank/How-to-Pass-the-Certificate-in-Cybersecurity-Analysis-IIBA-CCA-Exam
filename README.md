<h2><strong>What Is the Certificate in Cybersecurity Analysis (CCA)?</strong></h2>

<h2><strong>IIBA-CCA Exam Knowledge Areas and Skills Tested</strong></h2>

<p>The CCA exam evaluates a candidate&#39;s understanding across eight key cybersecurity knowledge areas that combine business analysis techniques with cybersecurity practices.</p>

<p><strong>1. Cybersecurity Overview and Basic Concepts &mdash; 14%</strong></p>

<p>This domain focuses on fundamental cybersecurity awareness and the role of business analysis in cybersecurity initiatives.</p>

<ul>
	<li>1.1 General Awareness: Understands the role of Business Analysis in Cybersecurity</li>
	<li>1.2 Practical Knowledge: Follows Rules to conduct a stakeholder analysis</li>
	<li>1.3 Practical Knowledge: Follows Rules using existing documentation to draft a RACI for a Cybersecurity project or program initiative</li>
	<li>1.4 General Awareness: Understands how to locate the organization&#39;s security framework or model, or know that one does not yet exist</li>
	<li>1.5 General Awareness: Understands what an Information Security Management System (ISMS) is and its objective</li>
	<li>1.6 General Awareness: Understands what data privacy is</li>
	<li>1.7 General Awareness: Understands the difference between an internal and external audit.</li>
	<li>1.8 Practical Knowledge: Follows Rules and knows the difference between compliance and best practice</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>2. Enterprise Risk &mdash; 14%</strong></p>

<p>This section focuses on identifying and managing cybersecurity risks within the enterprise environment.</p>

<ul>
	<li>2.1 General Awareness: Understands what a cyber risk is</li>
	<li>2.2 General Awareness: Basic Knowledge of what a Cybersecurity Risk Assessment is</li>
	<li>2.3 Practical Knowledge: Follows Rules for the inputs to a Business Case that BAs are typically responsible for</li>
	<li>2.4 General Awareness: Understands what Disaster Recovery Plans and Business Continuity Plans are</li>
	<li>2.5 Practical Knowledge: Follows Rules to develop a business process flow diagram, and identify steps along the path that present potential cybersecurity vulnerabilities</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>3. Cybersecurity Risks and Controls &mdash; 12%</strong></p>

<p>Candidates must understand common cybersecurity threats and the controls used to mitigate them.</p>

<ul>
	<li>3.1 General Awareness: Understands what Cybersecurity Controls are and where to find various versions</li>
	<li>3.2 General Awareness: Understands the three attributes of secure information: confidentiality, integrity and availability</li>
	<li>3.3 General Awareness: Understands the difference between a cyber threat and a cyber vulnerability</li>
	<li>3.4 Practical Knowledge: Follows Rules to identify typical impacts of a cyber-attack to an organization</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>4. Securing the Layers &mdash; 5%</strong></p>

<p>This domain focuses on understanding the different technology layers that must be protected.</p>

<ul>
	<li>4.1 General Awareness: Understands that there are multiple layers of technology to protect</li>
	<li>4.2 General Awareness: Understands what is meant by Endpoint Security</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>5. Data Security &mdash; 15%</strong></p>

<p>Data protection is a major focus of cybersecurity initiatives.</p>

<ul>
	<li>5.1 General Awareness: Understands what Information Classification means</li>
	<li>5.2 General Awareness: Understands what Information Categorization means</li>
	<li>5.3 General Awareness: Understands what Data Security at Rest means</li>
	<li>5.4 General Awareness: Understands what Data Security in Transit means</li>
	<li>5.5 General Awareness: Understands what Encryption is</li>
	<li>5.6 General Awareness: Understands what a Digital Signature is</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>6. User Access Control &mdash; 15%</strong></p>

<p>User access management ensures that only authorized individuals can access sensitive systems and information.</p>

<ul>
	<li>6.1 Practical Knowledge: Follows Rules to set up authorization</li>
	<li>6.2 General Awareness: Understands what authentication is</li>
	<li>6.3 General Awareness: Understands what access control means</li>
	<li>6.4 General Awareness: Understands what Privileged Account Management is</li>
